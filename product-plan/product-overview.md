# Stack and Scale — Product Overview

## Summary

Stack and Scale is a website that positions David Chancogne as a hands-on senior engineer and engineering leader who helps early-stage startups and mid-market companies build and scale their technology. From deep technical work (cloud infrastructure, AI/ML) to growing engineering teams from 10–50 people, Stack and Scale offers the blend of execution and leadership that young companies need.

## Planned Sections

1. **Home** — Introduction to David and Stack and Scale — establishing the core positioning as a hands-on senior engineer and leader who helps companies build and scale their technology and teams.
2. **Services** — Overview of the four core service offerings — technical consulting, full-time CTO, fractional CTO, and engineering leadership — with clarity on who each is for and what to expect.
3. **Experience** — Thematic highlights of David's key wins — demonstrating the ability to scale engineering orgs, modernize technical infrastructure, and lead companies through growth stages.
4. **Contact & Engage** — Simple contact form to capture inbound inquiries, including name, email, message, and optional engagement type preference.

## Product Entities

- **Service** — A service offering (Technical Consulting, Full-time CTO, Fractional CTO, Engineering Leadership)
- **Experience** — A thematic area of expertise shown as a card with icon, title, and description
- **Inquiry** — A contact form submission with name, email, message, and optional engagement type reference

## Implementation Sequence

Build this product in milestones:

1. **Shell** — Set up design tokens and application shell (sidebar nav, mobile hamburger)
2. **Home** — Hero landing page with headline, subheading, CTAs, and highlights grid
3. **Services** — Expandable service cards for the four engagement types
4. **Experience** — Thematic card grid showcasing seven expertise areas
5. **Contact & Engage** — Controlled contact form with validation and success state

Each milestone has a dedicated instruction document in `product-plan/instructions/incremental/`.
